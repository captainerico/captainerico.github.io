Kenyan Beef Samosas

Contents

  1. Filling ingredients
  2. Wrapping ingredients
  3. Preparation
  • Time: 50 minutes
  • Serves: 15

Recipe#1 Filling

Ingredients Quantity
olive oil 1 1/2 tablespoons
ground beef 455 g
medium red onion, diced 1
clove garlic, minced 1
jalapeno, minced 1
salt 1 teaspoon
pepper 1 teaspoon
ground coriander 1 teaspoon
cumin 1 teaspoon
peas 1 cup(150g)
scallions, diced 4
fresh cilantro, chopped 2 tablespoons

Recipe#2 Wrapping

Ingredients Quantity
all-purpose flour 3 cups (375g)
salt 1/4 teaspoon
Olive oil 2 teaspoons
water 1 cup(240ml) + two table
spoons more
1 tablespoon flour,
mixed with 1 tablespoon
of water.
canola oil 3 cups(720ml)
lime wedge, for serving

Preparation

Step 1:In a large pan over medium-high heat, heat the olive oil. Once hot, add the ground beef. Use a spatula to break apart the meat and cook for 3 minutes. Add onion and cook until beef is brown and onion is translucent, about 4 minutes.

Step 2: Add the garlic, jalapeño, salt, pepper, coriander, cumin, and scallions to the meat. Cook for 2 minutes. Add the peas and cilantro, then cook for another 2 minutes. Remove the meat filling from the heat and let cool.

Step 3: In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, olive oil, and water. Use your hand or a spatula to mix together. Once the dough pulls away from sides of the bowl, transfer to a lightly floured surface and knead for 4 minutes or until the dough is smooth and soft.

Step 4: Shape dough into a ball and brush with olive oil. Cover with plastic wrap and a warm damp tea towel. Let rest for 30 minutes.

Step 5: Remove tea towel and plastic wrap and roll the dough out into a 10-inch (25-cm) long log. Cut into 10 even sections. Take one section and roll it into a ball. Place the ball on a lightly floured surface and use a rolling pin to roll it out into a thin circle about 10 inches (25-cm) in diameter. Repeat with the remaining dough. Stack the dough circles as you roll them out, sprinkling a bit of flour between each one and keeping them covered with plastic wrap so they don't dry out.

Step 6: In a greased large pan over high heat, place one of the circles of dough and cook for 10 seconds on each side. Repeat with remaining dough.

Step 7: Cut one of the par-cooked dough circles into four quarters. Take one quarter and, with the round side closest to you, fold the bottom half to the right. Use the flour paste to seal the fold. Repeat with the left side, crossing over your previous fold to create a cone like pocket. Fill the pocket with meat filling, then fold the remaining tip of the dough towards you and seal with flour paste. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.

Step 8: In a large, deep pan, heat the canola oil to 350°F (180°C). Once the oil is at the correct temperature, carefully place 4-6 sambusas in the pan. Fry for about 5 minutes, flipping once, until light, golden brown. Remove from oil and drain on paper towels.
